Why is Diwali Celebrated?

Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is celebrated to mark the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. It is associated with Lord Rama's return to Ayodhya after defeating Ravana.

When is Diwali Celebrated?

Diwali is celebrated in October or November, depending on the Hindu lunar calendar. It falls on the new moon night (Amavasya) of the Kartika month.

What Do We Do on This Day?

  • Decorate homes with lamps and rangoli.
  • Perform Lakshmi Puja for prosperity.
  • Exchange sweets and gifts with loved ones.
  • Enjoy fireworks and celebrations.
